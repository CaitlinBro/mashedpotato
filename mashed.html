Why I Love Mashed Potatoes

As I'm sure you're aware, there are infinitely many ways that one can consume a potato.

For as long as I can remember whenever anyone asked me what my favorite food was, the answer has always been Mashed Potatoes.

Not all mashed potatoes are made equally which is why it's important to use trial and error and figure out what works for you!

I personally enjoy the Idahoan Mashed Potatoes (my dad refers to them as "fake potatoes"), however you may want something different! Luckily for us, there is no wrong way to mash a potato.

History

Why Mashed? Why Now?

Potatoes originated in present day Peru. Ancient cooking methods included Huatia, an oven created by clumps of dried earth, heated inside and then tossing in various tubers and other foodstuffs to "bake" inside. After introducing the foodstuffs the "oven" was flattened and cooking continued until there was evidence that the foodstuffs were done. In this case potatoes. There is no specific evidence, other than rational inference, that potatoes thus cooked may have been mashed, particularly to feed infants. To this day ethnic Peruvians refer to "papa" the same name as potatoes in Quechua and Spanish, as a general name for baby food (source)
